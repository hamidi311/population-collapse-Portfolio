Consequences of Population Collapse

Exploring the impacts of shrinking populations across the globe.

Population Over 65

These two charts highlight the aging population trend worldwide. The line chart shows a steady global increase in the number of people aged 65 and older, pointing to improved life expectancy and declining birth rates. The bar chart reveals the top 10 countries with the highest share of elderly population in 2022, led by Japan and Italy â€” both of which face critical challenges in healthcare, pensions, and workforce sustainability.

Net Migration Trends

This visualization illustrates net migration flows in selected countries from 2020 to 2028. High-income nations like the United States rely on migration to stabilize workforce numbers and counteract aging demographics. In contrast, countries with lower net migration may face steeper economic challenges tied to population decline.

AI Usage for Workforce Gaps

This chart compares AI adoption between 2021 and 2024 across global regions. Many aging societies are turning to automation and artificial intelligence to replace shrinking labor forces. Higher AI usage in North America, Europe, and East Asia reflects how technology is being used as a strategy to maintain productivity amid demographic shifts.
