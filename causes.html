Causes of Population Collapse

Understanding the demographic and societal triggers behind declining populations.

Religion and Population Growth

This chart compares the top 5 and bottom 5 countries by population growth while also highlighting their majority religions. Notably, countries with higher growth tend to have majority Muslim populations, whereas many Christian-majority nations appear among the lowest growth group. This reflects how cultural and religious norms may influence fertility and family size trends globally.

Workforce vs Fertility

This scatterplot shows the inverse relationship between female labor force participation and fertility rates in developed countries such as the U.S., France, and Germany. As more women enter the workforce, fertility rates tend to decline, suggesting that economic and social pressures may influence family planning decisions.

Top & Bottom 15 Fertility Rates

This heatmap highlights the countries with the highest and lowest fertility rates worldwide. Countries in sub-Saharan Africa dominate the top of the list with significantly higher fertility rates, while developed countries such as South Korea, Italy, and Japan appear at the bottom, underscoring a key demographic divide that contributes to population decline in wealthy nations.
